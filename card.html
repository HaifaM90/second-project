Raccoon

The raccoon is the largest of the procyonid family, Its grayish coat mostly consists of dense underfur which insulates it against cold weather.

  • Scientific name: Procyon lotor
  • Lifespan: 2 – 3 years (In the wild)
  • Height: 23 – 30 cm
  • Mass: 3.5 – 9 kg

The raccoons described as "clever beasts", Raccoons were able to open 11 of 13 complex locks in fewer than 10 tries and had no problems repeating the action when the locks were rearranged or turned upside down
